1//! # oxiflow
2//!
3//! Generic numerical engine for transport, reaction, and diffusion problems.
4//!
5//! ## The canonical form
6//!
7//! Every problem solved by oxiflow is expressed as a conservation law or field
8//! equation of the form:
9//!
10//! $$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot F(u, \nabla u) = S(u, \mathbf{x}, t)$$
11//!
12//! where:
13//!
14//! - **`u(x, t)`** — the primary field: concentration, temperature, velocity,
15//!   pressure, magnetic flux density, or any other quantity that evolves in
16//!   time and space.
17//! - **`F(u, ∇u)`** — the flux tensor, which may combine:
18//!   - *advective* flux `v·u` (transport by a flow field),
19//!   - *diffusive* flux `-D·∇u` (Fick, Fourier, Darcy),
20//!   - *dispersive* flux (higher-order spreading, e.g. in chromatography).
21//! - **`S(u, x, t)`** — source or reaction term: chemical reactions, heat
22//!   generation, gravity, electromagnetic forcing, adsorption isotherms.
23//!
24//! Second-order-in-time problems (`∂²u/∂t²`, structural dynamics, wave
25//! propagation) are reduced to this form by introducing `v = ∂u/∂t` as a
26//! second field component: the state `(u, v)` evolves as a first-order
27//! system. Newmark-β and HHT-α integrators are planned for J4+.
28//!
29//! ## Physical domains
30//!
31//! oxiflow covers any problem expressible as the canonical form above.
32//!
33//! | Domain | Primary field `u` | Milestone |
34//! |---|---|---|
35//! | Chromatographic transport | concentration `c(z,t)` | J1 |
36//! | Transient heat conduction | temperature `T(x,t)` | J2+ |
37//! | Reaction-diffusion | species `(u,v)` | J2+ |
38//! | Flow in porous media (Darcy) | water content `θ` | J2+ |
39//! | Shallow water (Saint-Venant) | depth `h`, momentum `hv` | J3 |
40//! | Electrochemical (Nernst-Planck) | ionic concentrations `cᵢ` | J3+ |
41//! | Resistive magnetic diffusion | flux density `B` | J7 |
42//! | Structural dynamics | displacement `u`, velocity `v` | J4+ |
43//!
44//! For full equations, physical derivations, and numerical details,
45//! see the **[project wiki](https://github.com/biface/oxiflow/wiki)**.
46//!
47//! ## Numerical structure
48//!
49//! In each domain above, `u` changes physical meaning, but the engine abstraction
50//! is identical:
51//!
52//! | Physical concept | Engine abstraction |
53//! |---|---|
54//! | Field `u(x,t)` | `ContextValue::ScalarField` / `VectorField` |
55//! | Spatial domain | `Mesh` (INV-1) — `UniformGrid1D` (J1), FEM (J7) |
56//! | Flux F, source S | `PhysicalModel::compute_physics(u, ctx)` |
57//! | Auxiliary quantities | `ComputeContext` ← `ContextCalculator` chain |
58//! | Time integration | `Solver` — Euler, RK4, … (J4) |
59//! | Boundary conditions | `BoundaryCondition: RequiresContext` (J2) |
60//! | Multi-domain coupling | `CouplingOperator` (INV-3, J3) |
61//! | Spatial operators | `DiscreteOperator<M: Mesh>` (INV-2, J4b) |
62//!
63//! ## Architecture — WHAT / HOW separation
64//!
65//! Three strictly separated responsibilities:
66//!
67//! | Type | Pole | Role |
68//! |---|---|---|
69//! | [`solver::Scenario`] | WHAT | Declares the problem: model, mesh, domains, BCs |
70//! | [`solver::SolverConfiguration`] | HOW | Configures solving: integrator, step control, calculators |
71//! | [`solver::Solver`] | Execution | Orchestrates the time integration loop |
72//!
73//! The engine enforces a **contractual execution order** at each time step:
74//!
75//! 1. Context calculators populate [`context::ComputeContext`]
76//! 2. Boundary conditions are applied to the current state (J2)
77//! 3. [`model::PhysicalModel::compute`] computes `du/dt`
78//! 4. The integrator advances the state by `dt`
79//!
80//! ## Modules
81//!
82//! - [`context`]   — [`context::ContextVariable`], [`context::ContextValue`],
83//!   [`context::OxiflowError`], [`context::ComputeContext`], [`context::ContextCalculator`]
84//! - [`mesh`]      — [`mesh::Mesh`] trait (INV-1) and [`mesh::UniformGrid1D`] (J1)
85//! - [`model`]     — [`model::RequiresContext`] and [`model::PhysicalModel`]
86//! - [`solver`]    — [`solver::Scenario`], [`solver::SolverConfiguration`],
87//!   [`solver::Solver`], [`solver::SimulationResult`]
88//! - [`boundary`]  — `BoundaryCondition` trait (J2)
89//! - [`operators`] — `DiscreteOperator<M: Mesh>` (INV-2, J4b)
90//! - [`coupling`]  — `CouplingOperator` (INV-3, J3)
91//!
92//! ## FEM invariants — forward compatibility
93//!
94//! All abstractions from v0.1 are designed to extend naturally to FEM at
95//! v2.0 without any breaking change on existing code.
96//!
97//! | Invariant | Guarantee | Active from |
98//! |---|---|---|
99//! | INV-1 | No `dx`/`nx` in public API — all spatial access via [`mesh::Mesh`] | v0.1.0 |
100//! | INV-2 | Integrators decoupled from spatial scheme via `DiscreteOperator<M>` | v0.5.0 |
101//! | INV-3 | Inter-domain coupling only via `CouplingOperator` | v0.3.0 |
102//! | INV-4 | All public traits object-safe — external crates can implement them | v2.0.0 |
103//!
104//! ## Getting started (J1)
105//!
106//! ```rust,ignore
107//! use oxiflow::{
108//!     context::ContextVariable,
109//!     mesh::{Mesh, UniformGrid1D},
110//!     model::{PhysicalModel, RequiresContext},
111//!     solver::{Scenario, SolverConfiguration, TimeConfiguration,
112//!              StepControl, IntegratorKind},
113//! };
114//!
115//! // 1. Declare the physical model
116//! struct MyModel;
117//! impl RequiresContext for MyModel {
118//!     fn required_variables(&self) -> Vec<ContextVariable> {
119//!         vec![ContextVariable::Time]
120//!     }
121//! }
122//! impl PhysicalModel for MyModel { /* … */ }
123//!
124//! // 2. Declare the problem (WHAT)
125//! let mesh = UniformGrid1D::new(100, 0.0, 1.0).unwrap();
126//! let scenario = Scenario::single(Box::new(MyModel), Box::new(mesh));
127//!
128//! // 3. Configure solving (HOW)
129//! let config = SolverConfiguration::new(
130//!     TimeConfiguration::new(600.0, StepControl::Fixed { dt: 0.1 }),
131//!     IntegratorKind::Euler,
132//! );
133//!
134//! // 4. Solve (J4 — integrator implementations land at v0.4.0)
135//! // let result = EulerSolver.solve(&scenario, &config)?;
136//! ```
